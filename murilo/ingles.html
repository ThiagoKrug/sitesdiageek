Jax Briggs

Name: Jackson "Jax" Briggs

Affiliation: United States Special Forces

Abilities: Jax possesses incredible strength due to his cybernetic arms. He is an exceptional hand-to-hand combatant with a variety of powerful moves, including impact strikes and grappling attacks.

Appearances: Jax made his first appearance in Mortal Kombat II and has been a recurring character in the series. He is one of the most iconic and respected characters in the Mortal Kombat universe.

About Jax:

Jax debuted in Mortal Kombat II. He is presented as an archetypal African-American hero. In the chain of command of the Special Forces, he is Sonya Blade's direct superior and the force that tempers Sonya's impulsiveness. Jax is clear, cautious, and always watches over what his team does. When the need arises, he becomes a beast on the battlefield with the attitude and ferocity of a lion. He unscrupulously uses the most advanced technology available to enhance both himself and his unit. He was responsible for disfiguring the criminal Kano, forcing him to wear a metal plate over part of his face.


In the alternate timeline, Jax died at the hands of Sindel and was resurrected as a revenant by Quan Chi, whom he was forced to serve. Although he was eventually freed and brought back to life, his experiences as a revenant left him traumatized, reducing the fierce warrior to a broken shadow of his former self. Later, he married a woman named Vera, with whom he had a daughter, Jacqui. Jax opposed the idea of her joining the Special Forces, as he did not want her to experience the same horrors and traumas he had. In Mortal Kombat 11, it is revealed that his wife had died, further breaking Jax and leading him to become a recluse. This gave Kronika the opportunity to recruit him for her goal of restoring the timeline, promising him the ability to prevent Jacqui from joining the Special Forces. Although he initially worked alongside Kronika, he was convinced to return to the side of good by his past self, his daughter, and Raiden, turning against his ally and joining the fight against her.

In the New Era, Jax starts as an FBI agent who co-founded the Outer World Investigation Agency alongside ex-Yakuza Kenshi Takahashi after helping him fight gangsters on the same night Kenshi first met Shang Tsung.

Abilities:

Jax is a skilled and strong soldier, known for his incredible physical strength. He can crush human skulls with extreme ease and cause ground tremors with a single punch. His strength is enough to create purple shockwaves and launch them at enemies. Since Mortal Kombat 3, Jax has used cybernetic enhancements that further increase his strength, including portable missiles, energy boosters, nanokinetic metal morphing, and a piston mechanism to amplify the power of his punches.

In later games, these cybernetics received new upgrades, such as rocket-propelled punches and plasma projectile launches. Jax also incorporates modern weaponry into his combat style, such as machine guns, grenades, rocket launchers, and RPGs, alongside his hand-to-hand combat skills. In Mortal Kombat 11, his cybernetic arms can heat up with each punch, increasing damage and allowing the launch of projectiles.

Besides his combat skills, Jax is a competent technician, scientist, and engineer. He was responsible for developing the portal technology used by the Outworld Investigation Agency and the SF's cybernetic enhancement program, as well as restoring Cyrax's free will after his defection from the Lin Kuei.


JAX'S STYLE AND OUTFITS:


The character Jax, from Mortal Kombat, is known for his striking appearance and combat outfits, which have undergone several changes throughout the franchise. Here are the main outfits and styles of Jax, focusing on the most iconic versions: Classic Outfit (Mortal Kombat 2 - 1993):

Primary color: Blue.
Clothes: Jax wears a sleeveless blue shirt with an emblem on the chest, showing a military style. He also has baggy combat pants and boots, as well as gloves covering his forearms and hands. Additional features: A kind of bandana or headband in some versions. Accessories: Combat gloves, with emphasis on his "cybernetic arms," which later became an important part of his look. Modern Version (Mortal Kombat 9 - 2011):

Primary colors: Blue and silver. Clothes: Jax now wears a combat t-shirt, combined with tactical pants and sturdy boots. His appearance is now more military and focused on a practical and realistic look. Cybernetic arms: Jax acquires cybernetic arms as part of his story, which are quite imposing and reinforce his appearance of brute strength. Mortal Kombat X (2015):

Primary colors: Black and gold. Clothes: Jax dresses in an even more futuristic military style, using a black tactical vest, pants with gold details, and boots. He also has his cybernetic arms, more advanced, with a more modern and futuristic design. Additional features: A black or gray bandana tied to the head, as well as shoulder pads that give a more imposing look. Mortal Kombat 11 (2019):

Primary colors: Black, silver, and red accents. Clothes: Jax appears in a more technical military outfit, still with a vest and tactical pants. His cybernetic arms have a more stylized design, with silver details and modern elements. Additional features: He also wears a bandana and, in some variations, more reinforced shoulder armor.
